## Overview

The greatest common divisor (GCD) and least common multiple (LCM) appear
throughout AMC 8 number theory and word problems. GCD tells you the largest
number that divides two values; LCM tells you the smallest number both values
divide into. Using prime factorization makes computing both quick and reliable.

## Key Ideas

- $\gcd(a, b)$ = product of shared prime factors with minimum exponents.
- $\operatorname{lcm}(a, b)$ = product of all prime factors with maximum
  exponents.
- $\gcd(a, b) \times \operatorname{lcm}(a, b) = a \times b$.
- **Euclidean algorithm**: $\gcd(a, b) = \gcd(b, a \mod b)$, repeated until the
  remainder is 0.

## Core Skills

### GCD via Prime Factorization

$\gcd(36, 48) = \gcd(2^2 \cdot 3^2,\; 2^4 \cdot 3) = 2^2 \cdot 3 = 12$.

### LCM via Prime Factorization

$\operatorname{lcm}(36, 48) = 2^4 \cdot 3^2 = 144$.

### Word Problems

"Two lights blink every 6 and 8 seconds. When do they next blink together?"
$\operatorname{lcm}(6, 8) = 24$ seconds.

## Worked Example

Find $\gcd(84, 120)$ and $\operatorname{lcm}(84, 120)$.

$84 = 2^2 \cdot 3 \cdot 7$, $120 = 2^3 \cdot 3 \cdot 5$.
$\gcd = 2^2 \cdot 3 = 12$. $\operatorname{lcm} = 2^3 \cdot 3 \cdot 5 \cdot 7 = 840$.
