## Overview

Factoring is the art of rewriting an expression as a product. On the AMC 8, the
most important patterns are the difference of squares and perfect square
trinomials. Recognizing these instantly saves time and unlocks harder problems.

## Key Ideas

- **Difference of squares**: $a^2 - b^2 = (a-b)(a+b)$.
- **Perfect square trinomial**: $a^2 + 2ab + b^2 = (a+b)^2$.
- **General quadratic factoring**: $x^2 + bx + c = (x+p)(x+q)$ where $p+q = b$
  and $pq = c$.
- Factor before expanding — it almost always simplifies the problem.

## Core Skills

### Spotting Difference of Squares

Numbers like $9999 = 10000 - 1 = (100-1)(100+1)$ use this pattern.

### Factoring Trinomials

Find two numbers that multiply to $c$ and add to $b$.

### Using Identities to Compute

$47^2 - 53^2 = (47-53)(47+53) = (-6)(100) = -600$.

## Worked Example

Factor and evaluate $101^2 - 99^2$.

$101^2 - 99^2 = (101-99)(101+99) = 2 \times 200 = 400$.
