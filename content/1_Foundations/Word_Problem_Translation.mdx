## Overview

The hardest part of most AMC 8 word problems is not the algebra — it is
translating the English into equations. This module focuses entirely on that
translation step: identifying unknowns, recognizing mathematical keywords, and
setting up the right equation before solving.

## Key Ideas

- Assign a variable to the unknown quantity. State what it represents clearly.
- "Is" or "equals" → $=$. "More than" → $+$. "Less than" → $-$.
  "Of" → $\times$. "Per" → $\div$.
- Read the entire problem before writing anything. Identify what is asked.
- Check your equation against the story: does it make sense dimensionally?

## Core Skills

### Identifying the Unknown

Decide what to call $x$, and write a sentence: "Let $x$ = the number of apples."

### Keyword Translation

Build a vocabulary table: "twice" = $2x$, "5 fewer than $y$" = $y - 5$, etc.

### Setting Up Multi-Step Equations

Some problems require two equations (a system) or introduce conditions like
"at least" (inequality).

## Worked Example

"Maria is 4 years older than twice Carlos's age. Their ages sum to 25. How old
is Carlos?"

Let $c$ = Carlos's age. Maria's age = $2c + 4$. Equation: $c + (2c+4) = 25$, so
$3c = 21$ and $c = 7$.
