## Overview

Defined-operation problems give you a made-up symbol like $a \star b$ and tell
you what it equals in terms of $a$ and $b$. Then they ask you to evaluate a
specific expression. These problems test careful substitution and order of
operations — nothing more.

## Key Ideas

- Treat the defined symbol as a function of its inputs.
- Substitute the given values exactly where the definition tells you to.
- If the operation is nested (e.g., $2 \star (3 \star 4)$), evaluate the inner
  operation first.
- Watch for non-commutativity: $a \star b$ may differ from $b \star a$.

## Core Skills

### Direct Substitution

Given $a \diamond b = 2a + b^2$, find $3 \diamond 4 = 2(3) + 4^2 = 22$.

### Nested Operations

Evaluate inside-out: first compute the inner result, then feed it into the outer
operation.

### Finding Unknowns

If $x \star 5 = 20$ and $a \star b = ab - a$, solve $5x - x = 20$, i.e.,
$4x = 20$, $x = 5$.

## Worked Example

Define $a \oplus b = a^2 - b$. Find $3 \oplus (2 \oplus 1)$.

Inner: $2 \oplus 1 = 4 - 1 = 3$. Outer: $3 \oplus 3 = 9 - 3 = 6$.
