## Overview

Rate problems are among the most common word problems on the AMC 8. The
fundamental relationship is $d = rt$ (distance equals rate times time). Most
contest rate questions involve two travelers, average speed traps, or
work-rate analogies.

## Key Ideas

- $d = rt$, so $r = d/t$ and $t = d/r$.
- **Average speed** = total distance ÷ total time (not the average of two
  speeds).
- **Meeting problems**: two travelers closing a gap — add rates if moving toward
  each other, subtract if moving in the same direction.
- Work-rate problems use the same structure: if a job takes $t$ hours, the rate
  is $1/t$ of the job per hour.

## Core Skills

### Single-Traveler Problems

Plug known values into $d = rt$ and solve for the unknown.

### Two-Traveler Meeting Problems

When two travelers move toward each other, the gap closes at the sum of their
speeds. Set total distance equal to closing rate times time.

### Average Speed Traps

If you drive 60 mph for one leg and 40 mph for an equal-distance return, the
average speed is not 50 mph. Compute total distance ÷ total time.

## Worked Example

A car travels 120 miles at 60 mph and returns the same 120 miles at 40 mph. Find
the average speed for the whole trip.

Total distance = 240 miles. Time going = 2 hours, time returning = 3 hours.
Average speed = $\frac{240}{5} = 48$ mph.
