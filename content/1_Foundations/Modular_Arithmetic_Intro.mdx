## Overview

Modular arithmetic is the mathematics of remainders. When you divide 17 by 5,
the remainder is 2, so we write $17 \equiv 2 \pmod{5}$. This notation and its
rules let you simplify enormous computations by working only with remainders.

## Key Ideas

- $a \equiv b \pmod{m}$ means $m \mid (a - b)$, i.e., $a$ and $b$ leave the
  same remainder when divided by $m$.
- You can add, subtract, and multiply congruences:
  if $a \equiv b$ and $c \equiv d \pmod{m}$, then $a+c \equiv b+d$ and
  $ac \equiv bd \pmod{m}$.
- Reduce before computing: $97 \times 103 \pmod{10}$ becomes $7 \times 3 = 21 \equiv 1 \pmod{10}$.
- Common moduli on AMC 8: 2 (parity), 3, 5, 9, 10.

## Core Skills

### Reducing Large Numbers

$2^{10} = 1024 \equiv 4 \pmod{10}$.

### Remainder Chains

Compute $7^{100} \pmod{5}$ by first noting $7 \equiv 2 \pmod{5}$, then track
powers of 2 mod 5.

### Divisibility via Modular Arithmetic

A number is divisible by 9 iff its digit sum is $\equiv 0 \pmod{9}$.

## Worked Example

What is the remainder when $3^{20}$ is divided by 5?

$3^1 \equiv 3$, $3^2 \equiv 4$, $3^3 \equiv 2$, $3^4 \equiv 1 \pmod{5}$.
The cycle length is 4. Since $20 = 4 \times 5$, we get $3^{20} \equiv 1 \pmod{5}$.
