## Overview

Geometric probability replaces counting outcomes with measuring areas (or
lengths, or volumes). The probability of hitting a target region is the ratio of
the target's area to the total area. This shows up on the AMC 8 as dartboard
problems, random-point-on-a-segment problems, and shaded-region problems.

## Key Ideas

- $P(\text{event}) = \frac{\text{favorable area}}{\text{total area}}$.
- The same idea works for lengths (1D) and volumes (3D).
- Draw the sample space as a geometric region and shade the favorable part.
- These problems often combine geometry skills (area of circles, triangles) with
  probability reasoning.

## Core Skills

### Dartboard Problems

A dart hits a circular board uniformly at random. The probability of landing in
a smaller circle = (smaller area)/(total area).

### Random Point on a Segment

A point is chosen uniformly on $[0, 10]$. $P(x > 7) = 3/10$.

### 2D Sample Spaces

Choose $(x, y)$ uniformly in a rectangle. Shade the region satisfying a
condition and find its area.

## Worked Example

A dart is thrown at a square dartboard of side 10. A circle of radius 5 is
inscribed in the square. What is the probability the dart lands inside the
circle?

$P = \frac{\pi(5)^2}{10^2} = \frac{25\pi}{100} = \frac{\pi}{4} \approx 78.5\%$.
