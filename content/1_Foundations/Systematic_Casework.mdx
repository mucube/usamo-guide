## Overview

Casework is the strategy of splitting a counting problem into non-overlapping
cases, solving each separately, and adding the results. The art lies in choosing
the right variable to case on so that each case is easy to handle. This is one of
the most versatile AMC 8 techniques.

## Key Ideas

- Cases must be **mutually exclusive** (no overlap) and **exhaustive** (cover
  everything).
- Choose the variable that creates the fewest, simplest cases.
- Count each case, then sum.
- Double-check: did your cases cover every possibility?

## Core Skills

### Choosing What to Case On

If a problem involves choosing digits, case on the leading digit (to avoid zeros
in the leading position).

### Organizing Cases

Use a table or list. Label cases clearly: Case 1, Case 2, etc.

### Avoiding Overlap

Make sure no outcome is counted in two different cases. Define boundaries
precisely.

## Worked Example

How many two-digit numbers have digits that sum to 8?

Case on the tens digit $t$ (where $t \ge 1$): $(1,7), (2,6), (3,5), (4,4),
(5,3), (6,2), (7,1), (8,0)$. That is 8 numbers.
