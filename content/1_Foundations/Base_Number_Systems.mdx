## Overview

This module covers two distinct but complementary AMC 8 topics. **Base-number
systems** teach you to read and convert numbers written in bases other than 10
(binary, octal, etc.). **Diophantine equations** are equations where only integer
solutions are allowed — and the key technique is bounding the variables to find
all possibilities.

## Key Ideas

- A number in base $b$ uses digits $0, 1, \ldots, b-1$. The place values are
  $b^0, b^1, b^2, \ldots$ from right to left.
- To convert base $b$ to base 10: multiply each digit by its place value and
  sum.
- To convert base 10 to base $b$: repeatedly divide by $b$ and collect
  remainders.
- A linear Diophantine equation $ax + by = c$ has integer solutions iff
  $\gcd(a, b) \mid c$.
- Bounding: if $x$ and $y$ are positive integers, use $x \ge 1$ and $y \ge 1$
  to limit the search.

## Core Skills

### Base Conversion: Base b → Base 10

$1011_2 = 1 \cdot 8 + 0 \cdot 4 + 1 \cdot 2 + 1 \cdot 1 = 11$.

### Base Conversion: Base 10 → Base b

Convert 25 to base 3: $25 = 8 \cdot 3 + 1$, $8 = 2 \cdot 3 + 2$,
$2 = 0 \cdot 3 + 2$. So $25 = 221_3$.

### Solving Diophantine Equations by Bounding

How many ways can you pay 50 cents using only nickels and dimes?
$5n + 10d = 50$, i.e., $n + 2d = 10$ with $n, d \ge 0$. Then $d$ can be
$0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5$ — six solutions.

## Worked Example

Convert $345_7$ to base 10.

$3 \cdot 49 + 4 \cdot 7 + 5 \cdot 1 = 147 + 28 + 5 = 180$.
