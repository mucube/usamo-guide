## Overview

Angle chasing is the process of deducing unknown angles from given ones using
geometric relationships. When a transversal crosses parallel lines, it creates a
predictable set of equal and supplementary angle pairs. Mastering these patterns
is essential for AMC 8 geometry.

## Key Ideas

- **Vertical angles** are equal.
- **Supplementary angles** sum to $180°$.
- When a transversal crosses parallel lines:
  - **Alternate interior angles** are equal.
  - **Corresponding angles** are equal.
  - **Co-interior (same-side interior) angles** are supplementary.
- The angles in a triangle sum to $180°$.

## Core Skills

### Identifying Angle Pairs

Label angles at each intersection and identify which pairs are alternate
interior, corresponding, or co-interior.

### Multi-Step Angle Chasing

Use one known angle to find the next, then the next, chaining relationships
across the figure.

### Exterior Angle Theorem

An exterior angle of a triangle equals the sum of the two non-adjacent interior
angles.

## Worked Example

Two parallel lines are cut by a transversal. One angle measures $65°$. Find the
co-interior angle on the same side.

Co-interior angles are supplementary: $180° - 65° = 115°$.
