## Overview

Proportional reasoning is the ability to see that two quantities change in a
fixed ratio. On the AMC 8 this appears in recipe scaling, map measurements,
similar figures, and rate problems.

Master these ideas and you will unlock shortcuts throughout algebra and geometry.

## Key Ideas

- **Direct proportion**: $y = kx$. Doubling $x$ doubles $y$.
- **Inverse proportion**: $y = \frac{k}{x}$. Doubling $x$ halves $y$.
- Cross-multiplication: $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}$ implies $ad = bc$.
- Scaling a recipe by factor $r$ multiplies every ingredient by $r$.

## Core Skills

### Setting Up Proportions

Identify the two quantities that vary together, write $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}$, and cross-multiply.

### Recognizing Direct vs. Inverse Variation

If more of one quantity causes more of the other, it is direct. If more of one
causes less of the other, it is inverse.

### Unit Rates

Divide to find a per-unit rate (e.g., miles per hour, cost per item), then
multiply to scale.

## Worked Example

A recipe calls for 3 cups of flour to make 24 cookies. How many cups are needed
for 60 cookies?

Set up: $\frac{3}{24} = \frac{x}{60}$. Cross-multiply: $24x = 180$, so
$x = 7.5$ cups.
