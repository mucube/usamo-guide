## Overview

Triangles are the most-tested geometric shape on the AMC 8. This module covers
the core properties: area formulas, the triangle inequality, and the special
characteristics of isosceles and equilateral triangles.

## Key Ideas

- **Area** = $\frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height}$.
- **Triangle inequality**: any side must be less than the sum of the other two
  sides. That is, $a + b > c$ for all three combinations.
- An **isosceles triangle** has two equal sides and two equal base angles.
- An **equilateral triangle** has all sides equal and all angles $60°$.
- The **perimeter** is the sum of all three sides.

## Core Skills

### Computing Area

Identify the base and its corresponding height (which must be perpendicular to
the base).

### Applying the Triangle Inequality

Given two sides, determine the range of possible lengths for the third side:
$|a - b| < c < a + b$.

### Isosceles and Equilateral Shortcuts

If two sides are equal, the base angles are equal, and vice versa. Equilateral
triangles subdivide nicely into 30-60-90 triangles.

## Worked Example

Two sides of a triangle have lengths 5 and 8. What are the possible integer
lengths for the third side?

By the triangle inequality: $|8-5| < c < 8+5$, so $3 < c < 13$. The integer
values are $4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12$ — nine values.
