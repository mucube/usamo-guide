## Overview

Many AMC 8 geometry problems ask for the area of a weird shape. The trick is
almost always to embed the weird shape inside a simple one (like a rectangle or
circle) and then subtract the parts you don't want. This "negative space"
strategy is one of the most powerful tools in contest geometry.

## Key Ideas

- **Bounding shape strategy**: enclose the region in a rectangle, triangle, or
  circle whose area you can compute easily.
- **Subtraction**: Shaded area = area of bounding shape − area of unshaded
  pieces.
- **Addition**: sometimes the shaded region is a union of simple shapes. Add
  their areas and subtract overlaps.
- Draw auxiliary lines to decompose complex figures into triangles and
  rectangles.

## Core Skills

### Rectangle Minus Triangles

A common pattern: a quadrilateral inscribed in a rectangle. Find the rectangle's
area and subtract the corner triangles.

### Circle Minus Polygon (and Vice Versa)

Compute the area of a circle segment by subtracting a triangle from a sector.

### Overlapping Regions

When two shapes overlap, use inclusion-exclusion: $|A \cup B| = |A| + |B| - |A \cap B|$.

## Worked Example

A square of side 10 has a quarter-circle of radius 10 drawn in one corner. Find
the area inside the square but outside the quarter-circle.

Square area = $100$. Quarter-circle area = $\frac{1}{4}\pi(10)^2 = 25\pi$.
Shaded = $100 - 25\pi$.
