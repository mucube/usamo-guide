## Overview

A prime number has exactly two positive divisors: 1 and itself. Every integer
greater than 1 is either prime or composite (a product of smaller primes).
Recognizing primes quickly and understanding the Sieve of Eratosthenes are
essential AMC 8 skills.

## Key Ideas

- 1 is neither prime nor composite. 2 is the only even prime.
- To test whether $n$ is prime, check divisibility by all primes up to
  $\sqrt{n}$.
- The Sieve of Eratosthenes efficiently lists all primes up to a given bound by
  crossing out multiples.
- There are 25 primes below 100. Knowing them by heart is a contest advantage.

## Core Skills

### Primality Testing

To check if 97 is prime: $\sqrt{97} < 10$, so test 2, 3, 5, 7. None divide 97,
so it is prime.

### The Sieve of Eratosthenes

List integers from 2 to $n$. Cross out multiples of 2 (except 2), then multiples
of 3 (except 3), then 5, 7, etc. What remains is prime.

### Twin Primes and Small Patterns

Pairs like $(11, 13)$ and $(17, 19)$ are twin primes. There's no formula, but
recognizing them helps on the AMC 8.

## Worked Example

Is 91 prime?

$\sqrt{91} < 10$. Check: $91/7 = 13$. So $91 = 7 \times 13$ — it is composite.
