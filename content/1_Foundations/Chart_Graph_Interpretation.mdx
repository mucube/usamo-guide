## Overview

Several AMC 8 problems each year present data in a visual format and ask you to
extract, compare, or compute with it. This module trains careful reading of bar
graphs, histograms, pie charts, and line graphs.

## Key Ideas

- Read axis labels and units before looking at the data.
- Histograms show frequency distributions; the height of each bar tells you how
  many data points fall in that interval.
- Pie charts represent parts of a whole; each slice is a percentage of the total.
- Watch for broken axes or non-uniform scales designed to mislead.

## Core Skills

### Reading Bar Graphs

Identify the category, read the height against the scale, and compare values.

### Interpreting Pie Charts

Each sector's central angle equals $\frac{\text{part}}{\text{whole}} \times 360°$.
Convert between angle, fraction, and percent.

### Multi-Step Data Questions

Some problems require combining data from different parts of the same chart or
from two different charts.

## Worked Example

A pie chart shows that 25% of students prefer soccer. If there are 120 students
total, how many prefer soccer?

$25\%$ of $120 = 0.25 \times 120 = 30$ students.
