## Overview

The extremal principle asks: what is the largest or smallest value something can
achieve? On the AMC 8 this shows up as optimization — maximize the sum, minimize
the product, find the best arrangement. The strategy is to push a variable to its
extreme and see what happens.

## Key Ideas

- Identify what you are optimizing and what constraints exist.
- Test boundary cases first: if $1 \le x \le 10$, check $x = 1$ and $x = 10$.
- For integer problems, the extremes are often when one variable is as large (or
  small) as possible.
- Pair the extremal approach with common sense and answer-checking.

## Core Skills

### Maximizing a Sum or Product

Under a constraint like $a + b = 10$, the product $ab$ is maximized when
$a = b = 5$.

### Minimizing with Integer Constraints

Find the arrangement of integers that pushes the target quantity to its lowest
value.

### Boundary Testing

When the problem says "at most" or "at least," test those boundaries.

## Worked Example

Find the largest possible product of two positive integers that sum to 20.

If $a + b = 20$, then $ab = a(20-a) = -a^2 + 20a$. This is maximized at
$a = 10$, giving $ab = 100$.
