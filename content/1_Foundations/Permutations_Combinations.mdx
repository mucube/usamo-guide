## Overview

Does order matter? That single question determines whether you use a permutation
or a combination. Permutations count arrangements where the sequence matters
(president, VP, treasurer). Combinations count selections where it does not
(choose a committee of 3). Mastering this distinction is critical for AMC 8
combinatorics.

## Key Ideas

- **Permutation**: an ordered arrangement. $P(n, r) = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!}$.
- **Combination**: an unordered selection. $\binom{n}{r} = \frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!}$.
- If rearranging the chosen items creates a "different" outcome, use
  permutations. If not, use combinations.
- $\binom{n}{r} = \binom{n}{n-r}$ (choosing what to include = choosing what to
  exclude).

## Core Skills

### Identifying Order

"How many 3-letter codes?" → order matters → permutation.
"How many 3-person committees?" → order doesn't matter → combination.

### Computing Small Binomial Coefficients

$\binom{5}{2} = \frac{5 \times 4}{2 \times 1} = 10$. Cancel before multiplying.

### Permutations with Repetition

If some items are identical, divide by the factorial of each group of repeats:
arrangements of MISSISSIPPI = $\frac{11!}{4! \cdot 4! \cdot 2!}$.

## Worked Example

How many ways can you choose 3 books from a shelf of 7?

$\binom{7}{3} = \frac{7 \times 6 \times 5}{3 \times 2 \times 1} = 35$.
