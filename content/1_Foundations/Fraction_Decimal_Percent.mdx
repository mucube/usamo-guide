## Overview

Converting fluently between fractions, decimals, and percents is one of the most
important speed skills on the AMC 8. Many problems can be solved in seconds if
you recognize that $\frac{1}{8} = 0.125 = 12.5\%$ without reaching for long
division.

This module builds mental-math fluency with benchmark conversions, trains you to
spot when a conversion shortcut applies, and covers common traps involving
repeating decimals and rounding.

## Key Ideas

- Every fraction $\frac{a}{b}$ can be written as a terminating or repeating
  decimal. A fraction terminates in base 10 exactly when $b$ has no prime
  factors other than 2 and 5.
- Percent means "per hundred": $x\% = \frac{x}{100}$.
- Memorize key benchmarks: $\frac{1}{3} \approx 33.\overline{3}\%$,
  $\frac{1}{6} \approx 16.\overline{6}\%$, $\frac{1}{8} = 12.5\%$, etc.
- "Of" in a word problem typically means multiplication:
  $25\%\text{ of }80 = 0.25 \times 80 = 20$.

## Core Skills

### Fraction → Decimal

Divide numerator by denominator. For contest speed, rewrite the denominator in
terms of powers of 10 when possible.

### Decimal → Percent

Multiply by 100 (shift the decimal point two places right).

### Percent → Fraction

Write the percent over 100 and simplify: $45\% = \frac{45}{100} = \frac{9}{20}$.

## Worked Example

Convert $\frac{7}{8}$ to a percent.

$\frac{7}{8} = \frac{7 \times 125}{8 \times 125} = \frac{875}{1000} = 0.875 = 87.5\%.$
